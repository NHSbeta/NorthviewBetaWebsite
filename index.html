A Beta Member's Goal

Here at Northview Beta Club, we are driven by a single goal: to do our part in making the world a better place for all. Our decision making process is informed by comprehensive empirical studies and high quality data evaluation. We strive to build productive relationships and make a positive impact with all of our pursuits.

Impact

The National Beta Club is the largest independent, non-profit, educational youth organization in America. And for more than 80 years, it has prepared today's students to be tomorrow's leaders.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the hour requirement for this semester?

The fall hour requirement is 8 Beta sponsored hours, and 10 non-sponsored hours. Extra Beta sponsored hours can count as non-sponsored hourâ€‹s. However, extra non-sponsored hours can not count for sponsored hours.

What is the difference between sponsored and non-sponsored hours?

Sponsored hours must come from the volunteer events or drives that officers send via email or GroupMe. Sponsored hours will be recorded on Northview Beta's google sheet. Non-sponsored hours can include volunteer events that you find yourself.

What are some examples of non-sponsored hours?

Volunteering at the library, church, or a club all can count as non-sponsored hours. If you also receive volunteer hours for another club or receive money, then the volunteering cannot count towards non-sponsored hours for Beta.

Where do we log hours?

Non-sponsored hours should be logged on your own, using the hour log found on the Volunteer Opportunities page of our website. We will take care of logging sponsored hours. At sponsored events, you should check in with an officer at the event, and they will log the hours for you on a shared spreadsheet. In case of an error, you should keep track of the event name, officer with whom you checked in and out, and number of hours volunteered.

Are we required to wear masks at in-person volunteer events?

Yes. As a Beta volunteer, you are representing Northview Beta when attending events, so it is imperative to wear a mask, act responsibly, and follow appropriate safety protocols. 

When are Beta Meetings?

You can find our meeting dates on the Meetings page of our website. We will also send out reminder emails before meetings, so make sure to keep your eyes peeled. If you can't make a meeting, you may be excused from one meeting per semester. To be excused, you must send an email by 11:59 PM the day before the meeting.
